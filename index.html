3. Size of the Drink

Dutch Bros offers different sizes for most of its drinks: Small, Medium, and Large. The size you choose impacts the price, with larger drinks costing more due to the increased amount of ingredients.

Example: A small iced coffee will generally be cheaper than a large blended energy drink.

4. Specialty and Seasonal Drinks

Dutch Bros frequently introduces limited-edition and seasonal drinks, especially during holidays or specific times of the year. These drinks often have a higher price point due to exclusive ingredients or unique flavor combinations.

Example: Seasonal drinks like Pumpkin Spice Breve during fall or Peppermint Mocha in winter may be priced higher than standard options.

5. Regional Taxes and Regulations

Local taxes and regulations also play a role in determining Dutch Bros prices. Sales taxes vary by state, and some areas impose additional taxes on sugary beverages. These costs are often passed on to the consumer.

Example: A drink purchased in California may cost more due to higher sales taxes compared to states with lower taxes.

6. Promotions and Discounts

Occasionally, Dutch Bros runs promotional offers, loyalty programs, or discounts. Customers can benefit from happy hours, app-based promotions, or loyalty card rewards.

Example: A “buy one, get one free” offer or a discount through the Dutch Bros app can significantly lower the price.

7. Supply Chain and Ingredient Costs

The global supply chain can impact the prices of ingredients like coffee beans, milk, and syrups. Changes in transportation costs or inflation can drive up ingredient prices, which may be reflected in Dutch Bros' menu.

Example: If the cost of coffee beans rises, Dutch Bros may adjust the price of their espresso-based drinks accordingly.
